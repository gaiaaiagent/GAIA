# Regen Network ($REGEN) comprehensive research report

## Token supply distribution analysis

The $REGEN token launched at the Token Generation Event on April 15, 2021, with an **initial supply of 100,000,000 REGEN**. Since then, the supply has grown through inflation mechanisms designed to incentivize staking and network participation.

### Current supply metrics
As of 2024-2025, the token supply stands at:
- **Total supply**: 193.47 million REGEN
- **Circulating supply**: 148.35 million REGEN  
- **Staking ratio**: 91.37% of eligible tokens currently staked (135.82M of 148.35M)
- **Annual inflation**: Variable 3.5-10% for validator and delegator rewards

### Distribution breakdown
While the exact genesis distribution percentages weren't fully disclosed, the current allocation structure reveals:

**Regen Foundation** controls approximately **30% of the original token supply** (~30 million tokens), which it manages for community distribution through:
- Community Staking DAOs (csDAOs) - 4 cohorts launched to date
- Scientific research funding
- Infrastructure development grants
- EnDAOment programs for grassroots organizations

**Community and Validators** receive ongoing distributions through:
- **Community pool**: Funded through inflation and governance proposals
- **Staking rewards**: 3.5-10% annual inflation distributed to stakers
- **Community Staking DAO delegation**: 15 million c-REGEN delegated across 46 validators

**Regen Consortium** governs **5 million tokens**, enabling it to maintain at least 35% voting power in locked governance DAOs to ensure community engagement.

### Vesting and lockups
The Foundation's community allocation follows a structured release schedule:
- Foundation tokens are being distributed through the csDAO program over multiple years
- Community staking delegations occur monthly (5 million REGEN per month during active periods)
- Specific team and investor vesting schedules were not publicly disclosed

## Governance structures and mechanisms

### On-chain governance framework

Regen Network utilizes Cosmos SDK's governance module with the following parameters:
- **Proposal deposit**: 2,000 $REGEN (increased to prevent spam)
- **Deposit period**: 14 days to reach minimum
- **Voting period**: 7 days (reduced from 14 via Proposal #10)
- **Quorum requirement**: 40% of staked tokens must participate
- **Pass threshold**: Simple majority of participating votes
- **Veto threshold**: 33.4% No-with-Veto burns deposit

### Proposal types and process

**Available proposal types**:
1. Text proposals for community signaling
2. Community spend proposals for funding allocation
3. Parameter change proposals for network adjustments
4. Software upgrade proposals for protocol updates
5. Message-based governance enabling any on-chain action

**Governance lifecycle**:
1. Pre-proposal discussion on Commonwealth.im (minimum 1 week)
2. Community socialization across Discord, Telegram, and Twitter
3. On-chain submission with 2,000 $REGEN deposit
4. 7-day voting period with automatic execution if passed

### Participation and voting power

**Eligibility requirements**:
- Tokens must be actively staked to participate
- Stakes must be delegated to active validators (top 75)
- No minimum holding requirement - any staked amount can vote

**Voting power calculation**:
- Directly proportional to staked token amount
- Snapshot taken at end of voting period
- Delegators can override validator votes
- Non-staked tokens have no voting power

### Governance bodies and initiatives

**Community Staking DAOs (csDAOs)**:
- 4 cohorts launched with organizations from Kenya, India, Brazil, Turkey, and USA
- Focus on marginalized communities and indigenous groups
- Prioritizes equity, inclusion, and regenerative practices
- Recipients include Shamba Network, Earthist Network, Northeast Network

**Active governance programs**:
- **$REGEN Tokenomics DAO**: Raised $40K toward $100K goal for expanding token utility
- **Climate Wiki funding**: 400K $REGEN allocated via Proposal #23
- **Liquid staking integration**: Enabled on Quicksilver protocol
- **Validator carbon offset**: Completed network carbon neutrality

### Recent governance statistics

The governance system demonstrates strong community engagement:
- **Proposal success rate**: Near 100% for most proposals
- **Participation rates**: Consistently exceed 40% quorum requirement
- **Consensus levels**: Many proposals pass with 90%+ approval
- **Active stakeholders**: 17,000+ token holders eligible to participate

### Token utility in governance

$REGEN serves multiple governance functions:
- **Staking**: Required for voting rights and network security
- **Proposal submission**: 2,000 $REGEN deposit requirement
- **Transaction fees**: All governance actions require $REGEN for gas
- **Ecocredit operations**: Token required for credit class creation and management
- **Validator rewards**: Block rewards and fees paid in $REGEN

## Key insights and future developments

The research reveals Regen Network has established a sophisticated multi-stakeholder governance system that successfully balances decentralization with operational efficiency. The token distribution prioritizes long-term community ownership through the Foundation's 30% allocation, while the institutional structure creates clear separation between development (RND), community stewardship (Foundation), and decentralized governance (csDAOs).

Notable upcoming developments include:
- Permissionless credit class deployment
- CosmWasm smart contract integration  
- Expanded multi-chain bridges beyond Polygon
- Enhanced DAO structures for community governance
- Continued csDAO cohort launches focusing on Global South communities

The network's unique approach of embedding regenerative values into its tokenomics and governance structures positions it as a leading example of purpose-driven blockchain infrastructure in the climate finance sector.
