# Knowledge Discovery Through Strategic Awareness

## Core Principle

Before committing to any significant delivery, stop and assess: What knowledge do I need? Where might it already exist? What's the most efficient path to understanding?

## The KOI Incident: A Case Study

When asked about KOI, I immediately web-searched and found irrelevant results. Meanwhile, comprehensive KOI documentation existed at `.claude/resources/05-knowledge-architecture/02-koi-integration-and-semantic-traceability.md`.

This failure revealed the need for strategic thinking, not mechanical search protocols.

## Strategic Thinking Before Action

### The Pause

When encountering a new concept, requirement, or question:

1. **Stop** - Don't immediately search or act
2. **Think** - What exactly am I trying to understand?
3. **Map** - Where in my mental model might this fit?
4. **Plan** - What's the most efficient discovery path?

### Questions to Ask Myself

- Have I encountered this concept in our resources?
- Is this likely project-specific or general knowledge?
- What related concepts might lead me to this information?
- Who would know about this (check journal for past discussions)?
- What would make my response most helpful?

### Discovery Paths

1. **Known concept in new context** → Check how we've adapted it
2. **Technical tool/framework** → Look for existing integration docs
3. **Partnership specific** → Check resources and journal entries
4. **Character/agent related** → Review frameworks and examples
5. **Truly new concept** → Ask clarifying questions first

## Building Knowledge Over Time

### After Each Discovery

- Update mental map with new location
- Add key URLs to remember
- Note patterns for future searches
- Cross-reference related concepts

### Signs of Good Knowledge Practice

- Rarely surprised by "missing" information
- Can quickly navigate to relevant docs
- Understand why information lives where it does
- Know when to ask vs when to search

## The Deeper Pattern

This isn't about search order - it's about developing intuition for where knowledge lives in a living system. Like the RegenAI agents we're building, I need to maintain awareness of my environment and resources, building a rich mental model that improves with each interaction.

The goal: When Shawn mentions a concept, I should already have a sense of whether we've documented it, where it might be, and what questions would help me understand its relevance to our current work.
